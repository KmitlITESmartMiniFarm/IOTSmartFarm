Cos Lettuce

      Cos Lettuce is a scientific name Lactuce sativa. Longifolla. General characteristics are biennial plants. Stacked into thin rounded leaves, cultured like leaf lettuce. But it looks. There is also a small rounded leaf wrapped in a sweet, crunchy, baby cos, which should be planted only in winter and rainy season.

      Cos Lettuce is a plant that needs cool weather. The optimum temperature is between 10 - 24 ° C in high temperature conditions. The leaf growth is reduced and the plant produces a milk-like substance or rubber, high fiber, sticky and bitter.

      Suitable soil for planting should be abundant. It has high organic matter, deep soil and good water absorption. The soil area between 6 - 6.5 area should be clear. The leaves are thin, not resistant to rain, so in the rainy season should be planted under the house.

Harvesting time: 45 – 60 days
Seeding temperature: 18 – 20 Celsius
pH value: 6.0
EC Value: 1.1 – 1.8

White Lettuce

     White lettuce is a family of Brassicaceae native to East Asia. Is a two years old plant. But the plant is a single year. It is native to northern China. There is a taproot system, but shallow, short stem, no branches. The leaves are white to light green. It is a single piece. The leaves or petioles are flat, with a white frame, very watery, the leaves are wrapped like a leaf. May be tight or loose with strains. When the leaves are actually 12 - 15 leaves can be eaten fresh. And to cook. The head is big. And the number of leaves. More than white lettuce sold in the market. And usually yield a lot in the winter.

Harvesting time: 60 days
Environment temperature: 18 – 20 Celsius
Seeding temperature: 10 – 16 Celsius
pH value: 6.0 – 6.5

Hydro3

Lets Focus on Content

I was browsing the web when I came across a tutorial on HTML5 Rocks and realized they have one of the coolest effect and subtle effects I have never seen before on any other blog/tutorial site. It is so simple and elegant I couldn't wait to try and replicate it. I would like to point out I did replicate it NOT it. I never even looked at the source code nor do I know how they actually accomplished their version of this. I do however give them full credit for not only the inspiration but the design. It is a really amazing and simple effect.

Alright, so what does this actually do. While if you notice to the left (if you are viewing this on xs device it will not show) we have a side menu that looks pretty ordinary. It will use Bootstrap's' affix plugin to stick itself to the top of the screen as you scroll down. After the side menu has gotten the class affix from bootstrap it will slowly start to fade out until you can barely see it. If you Hover over it or back to the top, you will notice it will quickly fade back into view. This very simple and small effect, helps users focus on the content of the blog, while still having an unobtrusive side menu.
